# Pogo Storm: Power Relay

## Refinement hypothesis

Three fast, automatically collected power relays with immediately distinct effects will make Pogo Storm's progression legible and give the player something exciting to anticipate without interrupting its bounce rhythm.

This child responds narrowly to the latest human evidence: **3/5 fun**, **22.4 seconds**, incomplete, with the note **“come up with power ups.”** That run reached skitters, hover weave, and the charger, so the encounter vocabulary is controlled. Because the run ended at second 13, the power comparison begins earlier and completes around second 11–12.

## The one changed dimension

Incremental orbit growth becomes a three-stop **Power Relay**:

- **Capacitor** launches around second 1 and makes every landing shockwave visibly wider.
- **Lancer** launches around second 6 and visibly auto-strikes the nearest threat on a steady cadence.
- **Phase Guard** launches around second 10, wraps the player in a gold guard, and turns the next enemy contact into a crush instead of damage.

The gold pickups home rapidly to the moving player. Collection never pauses, opens a menu, or asks for another input. Each pickup has its own silhouette and label, immediately demonstrates or arms its effect, and remains readable in the compact three-slot HUD.

## Controlled dimensions

Automatic pogo relaunch, Left/Right air steering, Space stomp timing, landing shockwaves, rough-landing/contact damage, five health, the existing ground-skitter/hover-weave/charger rotation, approximate enemy density and pressure, the 84-second run, deterministic seed 1729031, 640 × 640 keyboard play, and grayscale-plus-gold presentation remain controlled. The visible body, halo, spring, boot, guide, guard, and weapon are local children of one authoritative player transform. Every moving threat has one authoritative transform and the opening skitters begin moving as soon as the run starts.

Telemetry stays small and treats exposure separately from effect use. `power_acquired` records each relay collection, `power_trigger` records the first demonstrated or armed effect, and end-state telemetry reports `powers_acquired` and `powers_triggered` arrays. The existing first-input, encounter-pattern, stomp, damage, death, goal, and round-end evidence is retained.
