# Pogo Storm: Three Beats

## Refinement hypothesis

Rotating three mechanically distinct threat patterns through Pogo Storm's controlled bounce-and-stomp loop will turn repeated arcs into varied route and timing decisions without weakening the original rhythm.

This child responds narrowly to the human playtest: **3/5 fun**, **22.1 seconds**, incomplete, with the note **“add more variety.”** The evidence also says the existing core is legible enough to preserve: the player began with Left, took both contact and rough-landing damage, and grew the orbit rapidly to four by second 17. This experiment therefore changes encounter composition only.

## The one changed dimension

The parent's uniform mixed spawning becomes a repeating three-beat encounter sequence:

- **Ground skitter rush:** converging floor packs make the player choose a profitable landing cluster.
- **Hover weave:** threats sweep horizontally across two arc heights, making air steering or an earlier stomp change the safe route.
- **Charge line:** a bright telegraph precedes a fast ground-lane sweep, making the player preserve airtime instead of landing immediately.

The opening skitters move as soon as play begins. Hover weave arrives at about 5.4 seconds and charge line at about 10.8 seconds, putting all three patterns inside the prior 22.1-second session. The sequence then repeats while keeping roughly the parent's density and pressure.

## Controlled dimensions

Automatic pogo relaunch, Left/Right air steering, Space stomp timing, landing shockwaves, rough-land/contact damage, orbit XP thresholds, local cascades, five health, the 84-second compressed run, keyboard actions, seed, viewport, and grayscale-plus-gold presentation remain controlled. Player body, halo, spring, boot, guide, and orbiters are local children of one authoritative player transform; every moving threat has one authoritative transform.

Telemetry remains evidence rather than a proxy score. Existing `first_input`, `stomp` decisions, `goal`, `damage`, `death`, and `round_end` events are preserved. A small `encounter_pattern` decision event records exposure to skitter, hover, and charger waves so the next 1–5 `fun` rating can be interpreted against what the player actually saw.
